The Route

We know every block between the bridges.

Our regular line runs the Vernon Boulevard waterfront, south to north. We also cover the blocks just off it — anywhere a dog can get from a doorman to a patch of grass in under ten minutes.

Hunters Point South Park

Southern end of the line — ball fields, the pier, and the first stretch of river view.

Gantry Plaza State Park

Past the old shipping gantries and the Pepsi-Cola sign, right on the water.

Anable Basin

Quiet inlet, fewer crowds — a good stretch for dogs who need a little more space.

Court Square

Dense high-rise blocks; most of our elevator-line pickups start here.

Queens Plaza & Dutch Kills

Under the tracks and just west of it — walkable in a loop back toward the water.

Queensboro Bridge

Northern end of the line, where LIC gives way to Astoria.

If your building sits within a few blocks of this line, we almost certainly already walk it. If you're just outside it, ask — we're often willing to stretch the route for a standing client.

How It Works

How a first walk becomes a regular one.

Meet & Greet

A free 20-minute visit with you and your dog, no leash yet. We talk routine, triggers, and the route.

We Match a Walker

Your dog gets one or two consistent walkers, not a rotating roster.

Walk & GPS Recap

Every walk is timed and mapped. You get the route, the duration, and a photo the moment it ends.

You Adjust Anytime

Add, skip, or reschedule visits with a text to your walker. No app required.
